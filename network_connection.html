Building Your Professional Network

Networking is a powerful tool for career development. It opens doors to new opportunities, provides insights into various industries, and helps you build meaningful professional relationships. Whether you're attending events, reaching out to alumni, or connecting on LinkedIn, effective networking can significantly enhance your career prospects.

On this page, you'll find resources and tips to help you navigate the world of networking with confidence and authenticity.

Networking Tips and Strategies

  • Be Genuine: Approach networking with authenticity. Focus on building real relationships rather than just collecting contacts.
  • Prepare Your Elevator Pitch: Have a concise introduction ready that highlights who you are, what you do, and what you're looking for.
  • Listen Actively: Show genuine interest in others by asking questions and listening attentively to their responses.
  • Follow Up: After meeting someone, send a personalized follow-up message to express your appreciation and keep the conversation going.
  • Leverage Social Media: Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with professionals in your field, join relevant groups, and share industry-related content.

Informational Interviews using the TIARA Method

Preparing for your Information Interview

What is an Informational Interview?

An informational interview is an informal conversation with someone working in an area that interests you who will give you information and advice.

Be sure to prepare!

  • Ask yourself (and have answers for) the “big” questions
  • Research the person, company, and industry.
  • Prepare questions to ask using the TIARA method

What is the TIARA method?

TIARA is a proven systematic approach to build likeability with other people and your guide for the Q&A portion of any informational interview.

T- Trends
  • Try to make trend questions specific to the company, industry or function for the person you are interviewing.
  • Example: “How are changing consumer trends affecting your company’s products?”
I - Insights
  • Insight questions slowly bring the interview down to a personal level. Ask your interviewee about their specific experiences, day-to-day work, and career pathway.
  • Example: “What is the most challenging part of your job?”
A - Advice
  • Asking for advice brings the conversation back to you.
  • Example: “What should I be doing right now to prepare myself for a career in this industry?”
R - Resources
  • Ask for tools, blogs, websites, and other resources that the interviewee can recommend. This begins to close your conversation while giving you reasons & opportunities to follow up with your interviewee later!
  • Example: “What resources should I be looking at to learn more about this industry/function?”
A - Assignments
  • Asking “assignment” questions can give you another reason to follow up with your interviewee and show your dedication to the field.
  • Example: “What kinds of projects seem to add the most value to your organization? I’d like to see if I can start doing that kind of work in my classes at UMSI.”
